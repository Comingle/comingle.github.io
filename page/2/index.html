USB OTG

The USB protocol operates in a host/accessoryÂ configuration. Your computer is a USB host. It’s the main device to which you attach other devices (such as a USB keyboard, printer, camera, or phone), and it’s capable of providing power to operate and charge those devices.

Your phone typically behaves as an accessory, expecting to connect to a host and be manipulated byÂ that host.

The USB OTG (On-The-Go) standard allows some devices that are typically accessories to behave as hosts, so you could, for instance, plug your printer in to your phone to print a document.
